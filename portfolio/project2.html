Problem Area:

 Long-distance friendships struggle with maintaining meaningful, consistent communication due to:
 - Geographical separation
- Busy lifestyles
- Lack of shared daily experiences
- Communication becoming sporadic and superficial

 Problem Statement:

 How might we design a digital platform that transforms infrequent, disconnected interactions into rich, continuous memory-sharing experiences for long-distance friends, leveraging AI to create personalized, engaging prompts that encourage regular, meaningful communication and preserve shared memories in an interactive, visual format?
Competitive Analysis
A detailed examination of BeReal, SnapChat, and Marco Polo to understand their core features, strengths, and weaknesses. This comparison highlights key differentiators and opportunities for improving user engagement and satisfaction.

Research findings :
Participants wanted features that encouraged them to remain in touch even when nothing particularly exciting has happened in their life; many felt unsure of how to continue a meaningful conversation when faced with distance or time constraints; frequency and quality of connections is of utmost importance

Participants reported that they use many different applications to stay in touch with people due to limited features (primarily for messaging, primarily for video, etc.). Different features equated to qualities like formality
.
Participants stated that certain applications like messaging or FaceTime tended more to one-on-one interactions, whereas other applications (Instagram, TikTok) tended more to public interactions. Even with group chats, participants felt that it was often difficult to include everyone equally in a conversation.
